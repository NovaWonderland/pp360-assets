The Opportunity

Champion Health has a story
the country has not heard yet.

A big part of Champion Health's mission is proactive: helping employees across the country keep their health top of mind and stay ahead of problems before they start. The CHAMP Plan delivers that, and as a byproduct it puts real money back in employees' pockets every pay period, at no out-of-pocket cost to the employer and no meaningful change to HR workflows. And the vast majority of American workers have never heard of it. That is not a product problem. That is a content problem.

The Product Is Sound
The CHAMP Plan works. The health engagement is real and the savings are measurable and verifiable. The structure is legal and compliant, and the benefit shows up in every paycheck.
The Market Does Not Know It Yet
HR directors, CFOs, business owners, brokers, and employees across the country cannot act on something they have never encountered in a format that makes sense to them.
The Proof Points Exist
Champion Health already has clients whose employees are seeing the benefit. Their numbers, stories, and reactions are sitting unused, and that is the most powerful sales tool available to anyone in the network.
Content Closes the Gap
What Champion Health needs is not more explanation of how the product works. It needs content that makes the outcome feel inevitable and the decision feel easy, for clients, prospects, agents, and employees.
What's Missing

Five content gaps.
One unified voice.

Champion Health has identified five categories where content either does not exist or lacks the consistency and quality the company's current momentum requires. Each speaks to a different audience. To be clear, this is not agent training. Champion Health already has that. This is the prospect- and client-facing content agents put in front of the people they are trying to win. All five gaps need the same thing underneath: a team that understands the product well enough to make the content actually do its job.

And two of the five carry the most weight. Prospects do not want to hear from the people selling the plan. They want to hear from the people on it. The testimonials and case studies below are where Champion Health's credibility gets built, and every other piece of content works off that proof.

01
Medical Benefit Explanations
Most people still do not understand how the CHAMP Plan works. Not because it is complicated, but because no one has explained it clearly in a format they can watch, share, and remember.
  • Short-form explainer videos (60 to 90 sec) in plain language
  • Employee-facing content: what changes in your paycheck
  • Employer-facing content: the numbers and how enrollment works
  • Explainers agents can send straight to prospects and clients
  • FAQ video series addressing the most common objections
  • Versioned by audience, so every viewer sees what is relevant
02
Credibility Core
Customer Testimonials
The most persuasive thing Champion Health can show any prospect is a real person, from a company just like theirs, saying it worked and showing what changed. Those stories exist. They just have not been captured.

  • Stories built around verifiable outcomes: pay increase, savings, experience
  • Employer voices: HR directors, CFOs, operations leads
  • Employee voices: real people reacting to their first paycheck change

  • Industry and company-size variety for maximum prospect relevance

Champion Health's leadership has a genuine perspective on what American workers deserve from their benefits. That perspective should be public. Right now it is not.
